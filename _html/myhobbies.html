Practice martial arts

I started learning martial arts at the age of eight and I've never stopped since. I hold the rank of a black belt third degree in Shotokan Karate Do, one of the oldest Okinawan martial arts forms. My first black belt was awarded to me by Sensei Kaze, One of the four grand masters of this art. I once was part of a national team of Karate, and I participed in many international competition including the World championship of Karate. Now days, I do not practice heavely like I used to, but I keep a healthy balance and get in my Gee at least once a week.

One thing that highlights my karate career is my suspension from the art twice by the governing federation and also, for political reasons my black belt was stripped from me by the Local fedearation, but I recovered all back thanks to the support of very kind and wonderful people in the art.

Windsurf in cool places

windsurfing is another hobby that I picked up at a young age and stayed with me until this day. It is one of those highly addictive activity. once you learn it and get to a certain level of mastery, you will never stop. there is an element of thrill to it. I had the opportunity to windsurf in Australia, Hawai, Tenerif, and many other exautic places. Highly addictive, and highly rewarding at the same time.

Where and when I winsurf Depends on the season and weather. I can be in the Balboa Shores during the summer to catch the San Pedro's winds, or I can be in lakes in the spring. The santa Ana's usually are a big season for windsurfers in the southern sky. California lakes are a great place for some great slaloms during fall and winter. I've tried to ride and chase famous big waves, but that was pushing the envolope for me. One Day!
